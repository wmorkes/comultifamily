The Northern Colorado Multifamily Market

Northern Colorado — anchored by Fort Collins and Greeley — represents one of the most compelling secondary multifamily markets in the Rocky Mountain region. Colorado State University's enrollment of over 35,000 students creates consistent apartment demand in Fort Collins, with an undersupply of purpose-built student housing pushing significant student renter demand into the conventional apartment market. That dynamic supports occupancy across asset classes and vintage years in a way few non-university markets can match.

Fort Collins itself has developed into a nationally recognized mid-sized city, frequently appearing on best-places-to-live rankings driven by its outdoor recreation access, craft beverage industry, technology sector employment, and quality of life. These attributes attract young professionals and remote workers who form the market's conventional renter base alongside the student population. The result is a diversified demand profile that provides resilience across economic cycles.

Greeley, located 35 miles southeast of Fort Collins in Weld County, offers a distinct investment proposition. The University of Northern Colorado anchors student housing demand, while JBS USA and Banner Health provide substantial blue-collar and healthcare employment. Cap rates in Greeley have historically run 100–150 basis points wider than Fort Collins, creating meaningful yield opportunities for investors willing to underwrite the market's characteristics. Our $36.8M Fox Meadows closing — a 138-unit partial LIHTC community in Fort Collins — is the largest affordable housing transaction in Northern Colorado market history.

Investment Trends in Northern Colorado

Institutional buyer interest in Northern Colorado has grown meaningfully over the past five years as competition for Denver product has intensified and yield compression has pushed investors up the risk-return spectrum. Fort Collins in particular has attracted both national REIT interest and regional private equity buyers. The market's supply pipeline is more measured than Denver's, providing better near-term occupancy fundamentals for sellers.

Affordable housing represents a growing opportunity in this market. LIHTC transactions in Northern Colorado require specialized expertise given the complexity of tax credit compliance, HAP contract transfers, and the targeted buyer pool these assets attract. We've closed multiple affordable transactions in this corridor and maintain relationships with the national and regional buyers most active in this product type.

Student housing adjacent to CSU is among the most consistently occupied multifamily product in Colorado. Purpose-built student communities and conventional assets within walking distance of campus attract a specialized buyer set that underwrites on a per-bed basis and targets assets with strong lease-up velocity. We have the relationships with these buyers to maximize competitive tension on student-proximate assets.

Common Questions

Fort Collins Multifamily — Frequently Asked Questions

What drives multifamily demand in Fort Collins?
Colorado State University — with 35,000+ students — is the primary demand driver, creating consistent household formation. Fort Collins also benefits from a growing technology sector, strong quality of life metrics, and net in-migration from Colorado's Front Range.
What are typical cap rates for Fort Collins apartment buildings?
Fort Collins cap rates generally range from 5.0% to 7.0% depending on proximity to CSU, vintage, and asset quality. Student-adjacent product trades tighter; suburban workforce housing further from the university trends toward the higher end.
How does Fort Collins compare to Denver as an investment market?
Fort Collins offers a yield premium over Denver — typically 75–150 basis points — with lower entry prices per unit. The trade-off is less liquidity and a shallower buyer pool. A well-run marketing process reaching Denver and national capital is essential to maximizing competitive tension.
